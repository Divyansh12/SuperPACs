About this Project


Users can search for SuperPACs or politicians. Our app will display visualizations that focus on how a SuperPAC's contributions to congressmen affect legislation. Our data come from ProPublica, the FEC, and Congress.gov.



The focus of this website is to create visualizations of how SuperPAC contributions to members of congress affect their voting on different pieces of legislation.

Users can also search for a particular representative, the visuals will focus on what SuperPACs are contributing and how that is reflected in the representativeâ€™s voting on legislation. As the goal of the site is to inform, other relevant information, such as more information about a particular SuperPAC or politician, will be either given directly, or linked to, whenever possible.

Tutorial

Understanding the Visualization


The visualization we use is called parallel coordinates. There are many nodes, organized into columns. The nodes in the first column represent SuperPACs; in the second column, they represent legislators. A line from a SuperPAC to a legislator indicates spending (the thicker the line, the more money was spent). A green line means the SuperPAC spent money supporting the politician, and a purple line means it spent money opposing them.



Using Filters

To add a node to the current visualization, type the name of a SuperPAC or legislator into the search bar and click on their name in the dropdown menu that appears. You can also type in a ZIP code to add the representatives for that area, or add all members of a political party at once by typing the name of that party. To exclude a node from the current visualization, find the filter corresponding to it in the sidebar (you may need to add this filter first!) and click the + on the left side, changing it to a -. To clear a filter you've previously made, click the trash can icon on the right side of the filter.
